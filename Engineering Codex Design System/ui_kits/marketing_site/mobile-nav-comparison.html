● 6.1 · MOBILE NAV STUDY · 2 PATTERNS

Two doors. One workshop.

Both options scaled correctly. Both meet 44px tap targets. Pick the one that fits how Readix readers actually move through the site — drawer for depth, tabs for frequency.

PATTERN A · 01

Hamburger drawer

● Strengths Full-width, large display-type menu items feel editorial — matches Readix's serif voice. Good when each visit goes to one destination.
● Trade-offs Two taps to anywhere. Discovery suffers; primary actions are hidden behind the burger.
PATTERN B · 02

Bottom tab bar

● Strengths Five primary destinations always visible. One thumb. Readers logged in for Live Briefs hop between Home / Library / Live constantly — this rewards them.
● Trade-offs 72px of fixed bottom chrome reduces reading area. Less editorial; reads more like a product than a publication.
